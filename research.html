Doctoral research

My thesis is focused on characterising the atmospheres of extremely hot gaseous exoplanets, also known as ultra-hot Jupiters. I code in Python and compare high-resolution spectra with models via the cross-correlation technique. I've specialised in emission spectroscopy to study the daysides of these exoplanets (chemistry and dynamics).

I recently analysed data from WASP-76b obtained with ESPRESSO (ESO VLT, Paranal Obs.), and discovered the existence of iron winds on its dayside. You can find the publication that I led here:

  • ESPRESSO reveals neutral iron emission lines on the dayside of WASP-76b

I was also PI of an observational programme that targetted the daysides of other ultra-hot Jupiters with ESPRESSO, and we are now analysing this data.

For the final stretch of my PhD, I will analyse exoplanet atmosphere's using high-resolution data from the new NIRPS spectrograph (ESO 3.6m, La Silla Obs.).


Pre-doctoral research

Before my PhD, I participated in two long-term research projects, that earned me one first-author publication each:

  • The massive star-forming region IRAS18264-1152: I investigated the morphology and composition of this massive star-forming region and uncovered powerful protostellar jets that point to the location of several protostars.
  • Determination of sulfur abundances in a sample of solar neighbourhood stars: I used spectral synthesis to model sulfur lines and determine the abundance of this element in the stellar atmosphere.
